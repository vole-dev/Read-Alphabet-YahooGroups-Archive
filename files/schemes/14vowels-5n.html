According to this analysis, there are three basic ways of representing sounds:  traditional digraphs, upper case unigraphs, and IPA
Sampa and Kirshenbaum notations use the uppercase for the short vowels.  Unifon uses the uppercase for the long or free vowels.

Which notation is best?  They are all ASCII notations.  notations that do not use diacritics are rare in dictionary pronunciation guides.
The notations are just about logically equivalent.  Most merge some of the ah-awe sounds.  GA substitutes the short [aa] for the short [awe]
Compare the multiple transcriptions of the Beautiful Princess story and the Gettysberg Address, then make your own decision.
